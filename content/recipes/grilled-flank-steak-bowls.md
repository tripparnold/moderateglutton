I love beef, I love switching up the cut of beef, and I love this sauce. Amber and I eat this weekly. It cooks quickly and doesn't require too many dishes if you grill the steak and microwave the green beans — which sounds lazy but is genuinely the move on a weeknight.

The pistachio herb sauce is the whole point. It's punchy, a little funky from the Calabrian chilis, and it makes the rice worth eating too. Don't skip it, don't rush it, and make more than you think you need.

### **Ingredients**

**For the steak:**
- 1 lb flank steak (grass-fed if you can — see notes)
- Olive oil, light coating
- Salt and pepper

**For the pistachio herb sauce:**
- 1/2 cup pistachios, rough chopped by hand
- 1 bunch flat-leaf parsley
- 1 bunch Thai basil
- 1–2 cloves garlic, minced or grated
- 1/4 cup good olive oil
- 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
- 1 tbsp honey
- 2 tsp crushed Calabrian chilis (from a jar)
- Splash of soy sauce

**For the green beans:**
- 1 bag green beans
- Hoisin sauce, to taste
- Sriracha, to taste
- Soy sauce, to taste
- Garlic powder
- 1 lime wedge

**For the bowl:**
- 1 cup medium-grain white rice

### **Mise en Place**

- Wash rice and get it started
- Mince or grate garlic
- Rough chop pistachios by hand
- Chop parsley and Thai basil

### **Instructions**

1. **Start the rice.** Get it going first — everything else will be done before it is.
2. **Light the grill** and get it ripping hot. You need high heat to build a crust on a thin cut. Don't compromise on this.
3. **Make the sauce.** Combine olive oil, red wine vinegar, garlic, honey, Calabrian chilis, and soy sauce in a bowl. Add pistachios, parsley, and Thai basil. Stir, taste, adjust. It should be bright, a little sharp, and have some heat. Set aside.
4. **Season the steak.** Coat lightly in olive oil. Season well with salt and pepper — it's a thin cut so don't go as heavy as you would a ribeye, but still be generous.
5. **Grill undisturbed** for 7–8 minutes, rotating halfway through if you want crosshatch marks.
6. **Flip and finish** to around 130°F internal. Flank steak is best around medium — below that it gets chewy in a way that doesn't improve with the cut.
7. **Rest** the steak 5–10 minutes. Don't skip this.
8. **While the steak rests,** microwave the green beans 4–5 minutes. Toss in a bowl with hoisin, sriracha, soy sauce, garlic powder, and a squeeze of lime. Taste and adjust.
9. **Serve.** Rice in the bowl, green beans on the side. Slice the steak against the grain, lay it on top, and drench everything in the sauce.

### **Notes**

- We've had flavorless flank steaks on multiple occasions. The grass-fed steaks from HEB are noticeably better — more flavor, better texture. About $20/lb as of now, and worth it.
- The green bean method is whatever works. Microwaving is easy on a weeknight and gets them to the right texture. Pan or wok works too.
- This works with skirt steak, hanger, or really any thin quick-cooking cut — just adjust the cook time.
